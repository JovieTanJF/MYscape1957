 Malaysia 

Introduction

Malaysia is a dynamic nation in Southeast Asia, renowned for both its distinctive geographic structure and rich cultural variety. It is a federal constitutional monarchy made up of thirteen states and three federal territories. With more than 34 million residents, Malaysia is the 43rd most populous nation in the world. The population of the country is a diverse blend of nationalities and cultures, greatly influencing the political and social climate of the country. The ethnic diversity of the country is enriched by the presence of Chinese, Indian, and indigenous people, with almost half of the population identifying as Malay.
